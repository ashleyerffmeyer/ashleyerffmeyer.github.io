About Me

Mechanical engineer turned coder.

After more than a decade in roles using my mechanical engineer and engineering management background in the defense industry, I transitioned my career into software engineering because I enjoy making unique customer experiences, products, and websites. I love working in an industry where user experience and technology converge. I thrive in fast paced roles where I can see the effects of my work immediately and make changes based on feedback and new innovation.

In my current role as a software engineer in the health care field, I use React, Typescript, Node.js, Material-UI, PostgreSQL, GraphQL, and Amazon Web Services daily to solve bugs and create new features. My technical skills include JavaScript, jQuery, HTML, CSS, MongoDB, Express, Kotlin/Java, Cypress, and Jest. While working on side projects, I’ve gained exposure to other tech such as Angular, Next.js, Netlify, and Semantic UI React.

Outside of work, I am passionate about life long learning. I use my strong problem solving and communication skills to get girls and women excited about STEM and the tech industry. I volunteer at several Kansas City Women In Tech events throughout the year, including serving as Coding & Cupcake’s Curriculum Director. In this role, I brainstorm technical concepts, such as computer science fundamentals, HTML, CSS, & JavaScript, add them to the Coding & Cupcakes curriculum, and develop code examples and content to use in presentation and exercises.

In my free time, I love to eat and run my way through my adopted hometown of Kansas City. I look forward to Restaurant Week and Rock the Parkway half marathon weekend every year! When I am not out exploring KC, I’m hanging out with my black and tan dachshund named Chunk.
